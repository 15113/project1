About Me

I am a "Full-Spectrum" Software Architect with 25+ years of experience, currently leading technical vision at Carnegie Mellon University. I hold a PhD in Robotics (Planning & Scheduling Agents) and specialize in Legacy Software Modernization—turning fragile, aging codebases into robust, scalable assets.

For Investors & Search Funds (Eucharistia Consulting): In my advisory practice, I help Private Equity firms and Search Funds de-risk software acquisitions. Investors often buy companies running on legacy PHP, Java, or .NET stacks, assuming the code is an asset. I provide forensic Technical Due Diligence to determine if it is actually a liability.

Core Competencies

  • Forensic Code Audits: Assessing "Spaghetti Code," Security Vulnerabilities, and Scalability limits.
  • The "Enterprise Trinity": Deep, hands-on architectural fluency in Java, PHP, and .NET.

Professional Background: My career spans the computing spectrum—from low-level systems programming and epidemic modeling algorithms to designing the massive Student Information Systems that power CMU. I view software not just as code, but as a long-term asset that must be managed for risk and return.
